GRIEVANCE REGISTER

HOW TO USE THIS REGISTER
  1. This tracker is managed by the Communications Manager. All matters are to be registered by the receiver. The Communications Manager will then assign the matter to the most appropriate person to resolve with a specified timeframe based on the matter.
  2. Ensure you read or refer to the Third Party Grievance Policy in order to complete the tracker correctly.
    Third Party Grievance Policy
  3. Definitions:
    3.1 Grievance

    A grievance is a formal complaint lodged by stakeholders alleging damage, specific negative impact or dissatisfaction.

    Grievances relate mainly to perceived or observed misconduct by Company and Contractor personnel during operations of the program.

    A grievance is submitted in the expectation of corrective action or compensation.

    *Grievances are confidential and should not be made public.

    3.2 Issue

    An issue is any matter or agenda raised by the community of material interest to them and defined as questions, comments, concerns, suggestions, contributions and inputs of local and other knowledge to the program.

    3.3 Complaint

    A stakeholder complaint is any matter raised by the community expressing dissatisfaction in the way SDP and its representatives conduct their operations.

  4. Grievance Register

    All issues, complaints and grievances should be categorised under one of the categories.

    The receiver of the matter should assess the nature of the matter and decide which heading it best fits. It does not have to be perfect as long as it is categorised.

    A matter may fall into multiple categories but choose the one that best fits.

Categories
  • Compensation & Payments
  • Land
  • Environmental
  • Social
  • Accidents
  • Code of Conduct
  • Safety & Security
  • Recruitment & Labour
  • Human Rights violation
  • Misinformation or lack of information
  • Business opportunities
  • Community investments
  • Others (briefly describe)
Timeline

Based on the criticality of the matter the following timelines have been assigned, starting from when the matter was registered:

  • Serious – to be resolved within 3 months
  • Not-Serious – to be resolved within 1 month
